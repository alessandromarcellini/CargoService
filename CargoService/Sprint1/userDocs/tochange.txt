esprimi meglio questa parte: Partly deferred. The terminal out_of_service state is part of this sprint's model (it is the robot-failure policy, questions 1 and 4); what is deferred to Sprint 2 is its sonar-driven cause (the sustained D > DFREE detection, event outOfService(none), declared but emitted by no component in this sprint) and the 'Out of service' display evidence.

esprimi meglio la parte what happen dello stato engaged_retrylater/engaged_window : Realization of committente question 3 (verified by TP1.3): a request received while engaged is answered load_retrylater(engaged). The answer is served in a twin state (engaged_retrylater → engaged_window). The 30 s window is not re-opened by serving a request (nor by evaluating a distance event): it is realized by a one-shot timer armed at the acceptance, whose late firings are filtered against the engagement start time (see the realization note in the Project section). The guard covers the engagement window, which is the only phase where a request can physically reach the busy service: the inhibition is an asynchronous dispatch, so the boundary cannot be disabled atomically and a request may be in flight in the very instant the system becomes engaged (with the Sprint 2 IOPort — a web GUI serving multiple customers — that race becomes the norm); without the guard the actor semantics would defer such a request and serve it as an acceptance at the end of the cycle, i.e. the queueing this policy forbids. During the trip, instead, the customer-facing answer required by the requirement ("retrylater, if the IOPort is currently occupied by a container") is produced by the inhibited ioport itself on the display: the port is dedicated to the operation exactly for that whole span, and the inhibition has propagated seconds before, so no request can reach the service from the boundary. Requests injected during the trip by clients that bypass the ioport are outside the boundary conditions of the contract (the TestPlans exercise the guard in the window, TP1.3). Planned evolution: in Sprint 2, with the IOPort realized as a web GUI and multiple concurrent customers, the answering duty will be moved to a delega front-desk (an always-receptive actor that answers retrylater while the worker is busy and forwards when it is free), giving full-phase service-side coverage without twin states. (Observation interactions are different: get_hold/preset_hold are deliberately served at quiescence, see the Test plans.)

esprimi meglio questa parte dentro hold model backtracking in analisi del problema : Reversibility. Should the need ever arise to model the hold again as a single, self-contained entity — e.g. if the business logic had to reason about space (choosing slots by distance, forbidding areas), or if more writers than cargoservice appeared — the unified Hold can be reintroduced without too much effort: the registry is confined behind the service, and the interactions of the system name stations, not data structures.

giustifica meglioo la scelta pull per osservabilità : The pull choice is the simplest thing that serves the Test plans (P5). The push alternative is not discarded forever: the display requirement ("show the current state of the hold") will naturally push the hold state towards the IOPort in Sprint 2 — another change that stays cheap while it lives in the model.
